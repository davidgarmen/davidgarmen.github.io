Does Internalization Increase Information Acquisition? (2023) Latest Version.

Abstract: Order flow internalization by wholesalers has increased in the last decade. Retail orders internalization decreases liquidity in lit exchanges, but it may also favor off-exchange liquidity provision to sophisticated investors. Information acquisition determines price informativeness and market efficiency, and whether internalization encourages or discourages the acquisition of information by market participants is an open question. We measure short and long term information acquisition finding that the proportion of internalized volume is positively associated with information acquisition at both horizons. We test our mechanism showing that the effect of internalization on information acquisition is stronger for those stocks preferred by institutional investors and by uninformed traders proxied by Robinhood users holdings.
